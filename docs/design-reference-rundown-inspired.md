# Rundown-Inspired Design Reference

AI-brief should learn from mature AI media products without copying their brand, assets, wording, or layout pixel-for-pixel.

The goal is not to reproduce The Rundown AI. The goal is to adopt the useful product patterns:

- A strong daily brief rhythm.
- Newsletter-style issue pages.
- Dense but readable content cards.
- Tool and guide directories that feel curated, not dumped.
- Playbooks that feel executable and follow-along.
- Editorial cover images and thumbnails as product assets.

## What To Learn

### Daily Brief Structure

AI-brief should treat each daily issue as a structured briefing:

- Opening context.
- In today's AI brief.
- Latest developments.
- Details.
- Why it matters.
- What to do next.
- Related Playbooks.

The product should keep the Information -> Judgment -> Action model visible on every issue.

### Directory Structure

Tools and Playbooks should not be generic blog grids. They should behave like practical directories:

- Category filters.
- Sorting.
- Cards with clear setup cost, action label, audience, and related Playbook.
- Visual thumbnails that help scanning.

### Playbook Structure

Playbook detail pages should feel like follow-along workflows:

- Breadcrumb.
- Outcome.
- Difficulty.
- Estimated time.
- Tools needed.
- Steps.
- Prompt copy blocks.
- Checklist.
- Validation methods.
- Risks and fallback options.

## What Not To Copy

- Do not use external logos, brand marks, or brand phrases.
- Do not copy images from any external AI media product.
- Do not copy article copy, titles, or examples.
- Do not use their subscription framing or reader-count proof.
- Do not pixel-match their layouts.

## AI-brief-Specific Direction

AI-brief keeps its own navigation:

- Home
- News
- Models
- Projects
- Skills
- Articles
- Courses

Briefs, Tools, Playbooks, and Learn are legacy aliases or future expansion surfaces in the current MVP, not primary navigation.

AI-brief keeps its own decision vocabulary:

- What happened?
- Why it matters?
- Who is affected?
- What should I do?
- How do I verify?

## Subscription Scope

Subscription, email capture, paid membership, reader-count proof, and login CTAs are out of scope for the next redesign tasks.

Home should have strong entry points to today's brief and directories, but it should not include an email form or Subscribe CTA until a dedicated growth task is created.
